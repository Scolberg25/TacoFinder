Corn

About 9,000 years ago, Corn was originally domesticated in Mexico by native peoples. Their selective breeding transformed maize into modern day corn. After corn was brought across the Atlantic Ocean, most wheat eaten in Europe was grown and exported from Turkey, giving the plant the nickname “Turkish wheat.” This nickname led many to believe that maize originated in Eastern Europe instead of in Mesoamerica.

Wheat

Wheat is believed to have originated more than 17,000 years ago in the Tigris and Euphrates river valley near what is now Iraq. It was spread all over the world and is now a crucial part of most cuisines.

Chili Peppers

Chili peppers are believed to have been domesticated more than 6,000 years ago and were one of the first cultivated crops in the Central and South Americas. Even though chili peppers are used a fair amount in Mexican foods, they are very widely used in South Asian cuisines being prominent in Indian and Thai dishes.

Tomatoes

The species was thought to have mainly originated in Peru and Ecuador in the Andes Mountains. In the early 16th centry, the tomato was introduced to Europe by the Spanish. The Spanish and Italians seem to have been the first Europeans to adopt it and use it as food.

Cacao

Cacao (Also known as cocoa), is used to make chocolate. It was thought to be used as currency more than 3,000 years ago by the Mayan, Toltec, and Aztec peoples. It is thought that humans first ate cacao in Ecuador suggest as early as 5,000 years ago.

Avocado

Avocados are native to Mesoamerica and are mostly still produced there as they require a steady climate. The top 5 avocado producers worldwide are Mexico, the Dominican Republic, Peru, Indonesia, and Colombia.

Cilantro

Cilantro also known as coriander is a pungent herb native to southern Europe. Many people use cilantro as both a flavor element and an adornment in Mexican dishes.
